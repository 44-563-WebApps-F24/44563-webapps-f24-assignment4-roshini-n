Roshini Naguru presents a Greek play by Sophocles.

Oedipus at Colonos by Sophocles

ANT

Father, this task is mine— Sink gently down into thy resting-place,

OED

Woe is me!

ANT

Supporting on this loving hand Thy reverend aged form.

OED

Woe, for my cruel fate!

[_OEDIPUS is seated_]

CH

Now thou unbendest from thy stubborn ways, O man of woe! Declare, what mortal wight thou art, That, marked by troublous fortune, here art led. What native country, shall we learn, is thine?

OED

O strangers, I have none! But do not—

CH

What dost thou forbid, old sir?
